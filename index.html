Balanced Bites: A Visual Digest of US Dietary Data

Elevate Your Well-being with Every Bite. Take control of your health by understanding the impact of your food choices—because what you consume, and how much, shapes your tomorrow.

Objective

Our study shows the nutritional components of various food groups. These charts represents a different nutrient (sugar, protein, fat, calories and carbohydrates excluding sugar) in terms of their content measured in grams. The visualizations aim to provide insights into the nutritional makeup of foods commonly consumed within the U.S. demographic, helping viewers to understand and compare the relative quantities of these nutrients across different food categories. Through these we aim to encourage mindful eating by highlighting the nutritional profiles of different foods.

Concerning Because...

In 2017, 11 million (95% uncertainty interval [UI] 10–12) deaths and 255 million (234–274) DALYs were attributable to dietary risk factors. High intake of sodium (3 million [1–5] deaths and 70 million [34–118] DALYs), low intake of whole grains (3 million [2–4] deaths and 82 million [59–109] DALYs), and low intake of fruits (2 million [1–4] deaths and 65 million [41–92] DALYs) were the leading dietary risk factors for deaths and DALYs globally and in many countries.

Daily consumption of sweetened beverages: A comparative overview by category

Short Description

WHAT the Visualizations Show: The visualization presents a comparative analysis of the average daily consumption of sugar-sweetened beverages across two selected distinct categories. It highlights consumption patterns and potential dietary trends and to see which categories of people consume the most sugar-sweetened beverages.

WHO the Visualization is For: Designed for everyone because sugar consumption is important for everyone but mainly the young peoples, parents,health enthusiasts, nutritionists, policy-makers, educators, and anyone interested in dietary habits.

WHY We Should Care: Understanding consumption patterns is vital due to the health risks associated with sugar-sweetened beverages. Informed choices can lead to better health outcomes and targeted interventions. Especially for young people, as their bodies and eating habits take shape.

HOW to Read the Visualization:

  • Bars: Each bar represents the mean daily consumption for a category, with height indicating the average number of servings.
  • Colors: Different bar colors signify distinct categories, aiding in comparison.
  • Values on Bars: Numerical values provide precise measures of mean consumption.
  • Dropdowns: Select two distinct categories to compare their consumption patterns.
